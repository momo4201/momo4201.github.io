About Me

I am a final-year B.Tech. student in Biomedical Engineering at the Department of Biotechnology and Medical Engineering, NIT Rourkela. I am currently a TEEP Research Intern at Prof. Kang-Ming Chang's Lab, National Kaohsiung University of Science and Technology, Taiwan, where I work on nonlinear EEG–ECG interdependence analysis for sleep staging.

Previously, at the Intelligent Biosensing and Microsystems Lab, NIT Rourkela, I worked on EEG visual-perception experiments, interstitial glucose dynamics during physical activity, and computational analysis of hypertension-associated clinical variables. My research has led to publications under review and accepted at IEEE venues, and I am the founder and vice president of the IEEE EMBS Student Branch Chapter at NIT Rourkela.

Activities

Notes from seminars, workshops, and conferences I've recently attended.

June 2026 · Neurogati Summer School

A Deep Dive into Computational Neuroscience at Neurogati Summer School 2026

This June I attended the Neurogati Summer School, an intensive program covering core computational neuroscience frameworks across sensory and motor systems, brain dynamics, oculomotor systems, the basal ganglia, and reward-based decision making. The sessions on brain–computer interfaces and non-invasive neuromodulation connected directly to my own EEG research, and I came away with a much clearer picture of how current BCI approaches are grounded in the underlying neural circuitry.

December 2025 · NIT Rourkela

AI and Medical Image Informatics for Precision Medicine — a GIAN Course

I took part in a GIAN course at NIT Rourkela on interpretable and generative AI for digital pathology. Over the course we worked through image reconstruction, disease understanding, and — what I found most valuable — the practical considerations of deploying these models in real clinical settings. It sharpened how I think about explainability in my own work on physiological signals.

November 2025 · NIT Rourkela

Behind the Scenes at the National Bioengineering Conference

I joined the organizing team for the National Bioengineering Conference at NIT Rourkela, coordinating delegate logistics while also attending the technical sessions. Seeing contemporary bioengineering research presented up close — and helping the event run smoothly at the same time — was a great introduction to how academic conferences come together.
